About Valle Scala

More than an eco-camping destination — Valle Scala represents a philosophy of living in harmony with nature while building meaningful connections with people from around the world.

Valle Scala is not just an eco-camping site; it is a transformative experience where community and nature meet in perfect harmony. Nestled in a dreamlike natural setting between the mountains and the Mediterranean Sea, Valle Scala stands as a refuge for those seeking to reconnect with the essence of life through sustainability, community engagement, and conscious living.

Our Strategic Pillars

Sustainability

Permaculture Philosophy: We practice regenerative agriculture that heals the earth while producing abundant organic food. Our gardens are living classrooms where ancient wisdom meets modern sustainable techniques.

Organic Food Gardens: Multiple garden zones producing seasonal vegetables, herbs, and fruits. Guests and volunteers participate in planting, maintaining, and harvesting fresh, chemical-free produce.

Ethical Principles: We embrace the permaculture ethics of Earth Care, People Care, and Fair Share. Every decision considers long-term environmental impact and community benefit.

Eco-Friendly Infrastructure: Composting toilets, natural water management, and sustainable building materials throughout our facilities.

Community

Communal Cooking & Dining: There is no better way to connect with the world than around a table through food. Our community kitchen brings together culinary traditions from every corner of the globe.

International Volunteering: Through Workaway and direct volunteer programs, we host travelers from all nations. "Knowledge will set us free" — we believe in reciprocal learning.

Collaborative Living: We believe in the power of work and collective growth to collaborate, grow, and have fun together. Daily work sessions create bonds that last beyond your stay.

Cultural Exchange: Evening gatherings, language exchange, music sessions, and storytelling nights celebrate the diversity of our global community.

Physical & Mental Wellbeing

Nature Immersion: Surrounded by cork forests, hiking trails with sea views, and abundant wildlife. Reconnect with nature's healing power through daily outdoor activities.

Workshops & Learning: Regular workshops on sustainable living, traditional crafts, foraging, natural building, herbalism, yoga, meditation, and personal development.

Community Wellness: Shared dinners under the starry Mediterranean sky, morning yoga sessions, group hikes, and collective celebrations foster mental health.

Digital Detox: Limited connectivity encourages presence, conversation, and genuine human interaction in an increasingly digital world.

Discover dozens of mushroom species in our cork forest. Join guided foraging walks to learn identification and sustainable harvesting techniques. The most surprising kingdom awaits exploration.

Volunteer Opportunities

Expected commitment: 5 hours/day, 5 days/week. Weekends free for personal exploration.

Garden & Permaculture Team

Work in our extensive organic food gardens applying permaculture principles. Tasks include planting, weeding, harvesting, composting, seed saving, and building garden beds. Physical work that connects you deeply with earth's cycles.

Best for: Nature lovers, agriculture students, farming enthusiasts

Community Kitchen Team

Cooking by and for the community. Prepare meals for 20-40 people using garden produce. Menu planning, food preparation, baking bread, and ensuring dietary needs are met. Share your culinary traditions.

Best for: Food enthusiasts, aspiring chefs, cultural exchange seekers

Eco-Construction & Maintenance

Participate in building structures using sustainable materials and natural techniques. Learn cob construction, timber framing, rocket stove building, and basic carpentry.

Best for: Hands-on learners, DIY enthusiasts, future eco-builders

Workshop Facilitation

Share your skills through teaching workshops in any area of expertise. Help develop educational programs, create workshop materials, and facilitate knowledge exchange.

Best for: Teachers, artists, skilled professionals, passionate learners

Communications & Media

Document life at Valle Scala through photography, videography, writing, and social media management. Create content that shares our mission with the world.

Best for: Photographers, writers, social media managers, content creators

Community Coordination

Help manage daily operations including welcoming new arrivals, organizing activities, facilitating meetings, and coordinating volunteer schedules.

Best for: Organizers, natural leaders, communication specialists

From Naples: A1 north → exit Cassino → SS630 to Itri (1h 15min)

Parking: Ample free parking for cars and campers

GPS: Enter "Contrada Valle Scala 49, Itri" — Final 2km is scenic unpaved road

Transfer options: Taxi (~€20-25), Hitchhike, or arranged pickup

Contact: Call +39 328 909 8856 for pickup arrangements

Pro Tips for Finding Us

The final 2km to Valle Scala is a scenic unpaved country road through cork forest — drive slowly and enjoy the transition from modern world to nature sanctuary. Cell reception may be limited on approach. Download offline maps beforehand. If lost, locals in Itri town know Valle Scala well — ask for "Valle Scala" We're happy to provide voice-guided directions by phone as you approach. First-timers often say finding Valle Scala feels like discovering a secret paradise!
